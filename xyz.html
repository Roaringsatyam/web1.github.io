Cubbon Park, Bangalore - A Green Oasis in the Heart of the City

Cubbon Park, established in 1870 by Sir Mark Cubbon, is a 300-acre park in the heart of Bangalore. it serves as a peaceful retreat for locals and tourists, offering lush greenery, walking trails, and cultural landmarks.

How To Reach

  • By Metro: Cubbon Park Metro Station (Green Line).

  • By Bus: Multiple BMTC routes pass by the park.

  • By Car: Parking available near the park entrance.

Main Attractions

  • Statues & Monuments: Includes statues of Queen Victoria and Sir Mark Cubbon.

  • Flora & Fauna: Home to diverse trees, birds, and small wildlife.

  • Glass House: A Victorian-style glass pavilion for exhibitions

  • Cubbon Park Library: One of the largest libraries in India.

  • Children's Play Area: Fun and safe for young visitors.

Things to Do

  • Walking & Jogging: Dedicated tracks for fitness enthusiasts.

  • Cycling: Bicycle rentals available for a scenic ride.

  • Photography: Capture nature, statues, and the serene environment.

  • Picnics & Relaxation: Perfect spots for a leisurely day out.

  • Yoga & Fitness: Regular outdoor yoga and fitness sessions.

Best Time to Visit

  • Ideal Months: November to February for cooler weather

  • Morning & Evening:park offering different vibes at each time.

Visitor Tips

  • Timings: Open from 5 AM to 8 PM.

  • Pets: Allowed on leashes.

  • Safety: Keep belongings secure and avoid visiting late at night.
